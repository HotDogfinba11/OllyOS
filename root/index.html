Rooting

Olly’s OS is designed for a good user experience out-of-the-box, without requiring modifications or any other changes. I do not officially support rooting or any other modifications to the system because they are prone to breakage and tend to cause issues. Rooting, in particular, weakens the security of your device and is often the source of many issues. However, I recognize that many of our users will root their devices regardless of this, so I’ve written this guide for rooting with Magisk.

If you have trouble rooting, you can try asking the community, but no support is guaranteed.

Installing Magisk​

Patching the boot and vbmeta images is the recommend way to root with Magisk.

Download the boot and vbmeta images​

The boot images are different for both devices, so it’s important that you download the correct one:

  • Pixel 3: Download the boot image.
  • Pixel 3 XL: Download the boot image.

Install the Magisk app​

Download and install the latest stable version of the Magisk app from GitHub.

Patch the boot image​

Follow the official Magisk instructions to patch and flash the boot image.

Passing SafetyNet​

Olly’s OS passes SafetyNet out-of-the-box, but rooting will cause SafetyNet to fail again. To fix this, enable Zygisk and DenyList in the Magisk settings and restart your device. After the reboot you may add “Google Play Services” to the DenyListy. You can also use the following command to add Google Play Services to the DenyList:

adb shell magisk --denylist add com.google.android.gms com.google.android.gms.unstable
